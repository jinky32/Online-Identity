  2. How has this Library been structured?

    This Library follows the priciples of Atomic Web Design.

    1. Atoms

      The building blocks of the interface. Eg. Basic typography (fonts, headings, lists), colour palate etc.

    2. Molecules

      Atoms combined together to serve as the backbone of our design system. Eg. A form label, input field and button combine to make a search box

    3. Organisms

      Molecules joined together to form a relatively complex, distinct section of an interface. Eg. A site header could be made up of a logo, nav bar and search box.

    4. Templates

      Groups of organisms combined to form page wireframes. Here we strart to see things like layout in action

    5. Pages

      Specific instances of templates. Placeholder content is replaced with real content to give an accurate depiction of what a user will ultimately see.
